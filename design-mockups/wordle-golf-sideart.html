Windmill Lane — 5 side-art concepts

The board stays the same; the art panel beside it reacts to every turn. Hit Putt! on any tab to simulate a full hole (3 turns → birdie) and watch the panel change. Fourth press resets.

Windmill Watcher. The course mascot is alive. Each guess spins the blades faster; good guesses light the window; solving the hole sends it into a full confetti party-spin. One landmark, escalating energy.

Ball’s Big Journey. The art is a map of the hole. Every guess putts the ball further along the winding path — through the tunnel, around the loop — and solving sinks it in the cup with a flag freak-out. Progress you can literally see.

Peanut Gallery. A crowd of course critters reacts to every move: the gopher pops up to watch, everyone gasps on turn two, and a solve gets a hopping ovation with judge score cards. The art is an audience.

Mood Sky. One scene, four moods. The hole starts at sunny morning; each turn rolls the day forward — clouds, golden hour — and solving lands you in a starry night with fireworks over the course. Calmest concept, very satisfying arc.

Obstacle Roulette. Classic mini-golf logic: every hole has a ridiculous obstacle, so every turn a random one crashes onto the green — T-rex, UFO, octopus, loop-the-loop. Pure surprise-generator; kids will putt just to see what shows up.
